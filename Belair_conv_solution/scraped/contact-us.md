## Why Are Car Insurance Premiums Rising? Factors and Solutions
**URL:** https://www.belairdirect.com/blog/insurance-premium-increases-explained

### Understanding car insurance premium increase: what you need to know

You’ve probably noticed that insurance premiums are on the rise everywhere lately. Inevitably, this has affected the car insurance and home insurance sectors, since the rates that impact your insurance premiums are based on current market conditions.

### What causes car insurance premium increases? 

### 1. Climate change

High winds, rain and hail storms, forest fires… Climate change has more than doubled insurance claims due to severe weather conditions over the past 5 to 10 years. This means insurance companies are reimbursing four times more claims related to natural disasters today than they did in the 1980s.

This is why, in the near future, insurance companies will need to implement premium increases for customers. These increases will allow insurers to keep pace with the mounting number of claims and thus continue to adequately protect policyholders.

### 2. Increased theft

In 2022, the number of car thefts may well be the highest we’ve seen since 2012. Most vehicles are stolen from shopping centre and supermarket parking lots—surprisingly, mainly during the daytime. Insurance companies have had no choice but to adapt to this frustrating reality, including by implementing insurance premium increases.

### 3. An increase in the price of parts and labour

Have you tried to hire a tradesperson lately to replace a part on your vehicle? If so, you’ve probably had to add significantly to your repair budget. Here’s why: inflation and a growing labour shortage.

Unfortunately, this has also affected the suppliers who perform claims work for insurance companies. We now pay more to obtain their services. For example, even though the number of claims only rose by 3.4% in 2020, repair work rose by 11.2%. It’s an increase that unfortunately leads to insurance premium increases.

### 4. Increase in driving

At the start of the pandemic, some insurance companies—including belairdirect—adjusted rates to give customers some breathing room. But with things being largely back to “normal,” as well as all the factors listed above, these rates are being readjusted to reflect today’s new reality.

For a broader understanding of why auto insurance premiums have increased, visit the Insurance Bureau of Canada for more information. 

### How to manage your car insurance costs

There are several ways to reduce the cost of your car insurance premium. Here are some practical tips to help you save: 

- Increase your deductible: A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. By increasing your deductible, you can lower your monthly premium because you are taking on more of the initial cost in the event of a claim. Learn more about how insurance deductibles work. 

- Consider bundling your insurance: Combining multiple insurance products, like car and home insurance, with a single trusted insurer like belairdirect allows you to save both time and money. Bundling can lead to significant discounts on your premiums. Discover our insurance bundle options now. 

- Install a home security system: Installing a central alarm system can lower your home insurance premium. It shows your insurer that you're taking proactive steps to prevent loss from theft or damage caused by a home invasion, which reduces the risk for the insurer. 

- Drive together, save together: If multiple family members in your household drive, insuring all vehicles with the same company can lead to discounts. This consolidates your insurance and often results in a lower overall premium. 

- Have winter tires installed: Winter tires improve your vehicle’s safety in cold weather conditions. This can lead to premium reductions, as insurers recognize the reduced risk of accidents.  

- Usage-based insurance: With automerit, you can take control of your car insurance costs. This program adjusts your premiums based on your driving habits, potentially rewarding safe drivers with lower rates. 

- Group program: Joining a group insurance program can offer additional savings. These programs provide preferred group discounts for your car and/or home insurance on top of any other discounts you may qualify for. 

- Reduce your mileage: By driving less, you lower the likelihood of being involved in an accident. If possible, use public transportation or carpool to commute. Reduced mileage can lead to lower insurance premiums because it represents less risk. 

### Understanding your policy and its impact on premiums

Understanding your insurance policy and how it impacts your premiums is crucial. Your insurance premium isn’t random but is rigorously calculated by an actuarial process and a host of other criteria: 

- Policy coverage: The extent of your coverage will impact your premium. Comprehensive and collision coverage, for instance, will cost more than basic liability coverage. 

- Driving record: A clean driving record can significantly lower your premiums. Conversely, a history of accidents or traffic violations can increase your rates. 

- Vehicle type: The make and model of your vehicle can affect your insurance costs. Generally, more expensive and high-performance cars cost more to insure. 

- Usage and mileage: How often and how far you drive can influence your premium. Lower mileage can mean lower rates. 

- Location: Where you live and park your vehicle also plays a role. Areas with higher theft rates or more traffic can lead to higher premiums. 

Remember, there are multiple ways to manage and potentially lower your car insurance costs. If you have any questions about your insurance premium or need a personalized quote, don’t hesitate to get in touch with us. We’re here to help you understand and optimize your coverage.
