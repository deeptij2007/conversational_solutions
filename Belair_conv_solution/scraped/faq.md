### Car insurance

### 	
				 
				 How do I customize my car insurance coverage to match how I actually drive?

Start by right-sizing your core coverages—third-party liability (called civil liability in Quebec), collision/comprehensive, and deductibles—to fit your driving patterns and risk tolerance.

In plain language

- Third-party liability: Covers damage or injuries you cause to others.
					 
- Physical damage: Protects your vehicle:

- Collision for crashes/impacts.
					 
- Comprehensive for non-collision perils (theft, vandalism, hail, glass).

- Deductible: What you pay on a covered claim before insurance pays the rest.
					 
- Usage-based insurance: Telematics aligns pricing to how you drive (a mobile/app system that records factors like speed, braking, and time of day). At belairdirect, automerit is the usage-based option, where available.

Availability and limits by province

Coverage options, minimums, endorsement forms, and telematics availability differ by province and policy. Program features and eligibility can change, and outcomes are not guaranteed.

How to confirm your coverage

Review your policy and any program terms, then contact a licensed belairdirect representative to confirm provincial requirements and discuss options—including whether automerit is available for your profile—to choose coverage combinations that reflect your driving.

### 
				 
				 Can I change my deductible (and does it change my price)?

Generally, yes—you can change your deductible, and raising it typically lowers your premium while lowering it typically raises it. Exact options and price impact vary by province, insurer, and by coverage (collision vs. comprehensive).

Keep it balanced

Pick a deductible you could comfortably pay on a claim without spending more in premium than fits your budget.

Next step

For deductible options in your province and how each affects your price, contact your insurer or a licensed belairdirect representative.

### 
				 
				 Do I need special coverage if I use my car for ridesharing, delivery, or business errands?

You'll need the right insurance setup when you use your car for ridesharing, deliveries, or business errands, and requirements differ by province. Ridesharing coverage is typically provided by the platform (e.g., Uber) during defined app-on periods—not by belairdirect—and you must keep an active personal auto policy with belairdirect to be eligible. Delivery and business use may require an endorsement or a commercial policy, depending on how and where you drive.

In plain language (by use case)

- Ridesharing (carrying passengers via an app): Platform coverage by ridesharing companies (e.g., Uber) generally applies only while the app is on; it doesn't replace your personal policy. Keep your personal auto policy active to maintain coverage outside app periods.
					 
- Delivery (food/courier/groceries for pay): Personal policies often exclude carrying goods for compensation. Depending on province, platform, and frequency, you may need a delivery endorsement or a commercial policy. belairdirect does not provide ridesharing platform coverage; confirm whether any platform-provided coverage applies to delivery and what gaps remain.
					 
- Business errands (occasional work trips): Occasional business use may be acceptable with proper classing; frequent or higher-risk business use typically requires a business-use endorsement or a commercial policy.

What to do next

- Confirm what your platform covers in each app period (app on, en route, passenger/delivery) and identify any gaps.
					 
- Keep your personal auto policy active to remain eligible with belairdirect.
					 
- Ask belairdirect (or your insurer) whether your delivery or business use needs an endorsement or a commercial policy in your province.

Quebec note: Carrying passengers for pay outside an approved platform and without a taxi permit is generally not insurable under a personal policy. Driving for an approved platform (e.g., Uber) is typically noted on the policy without a special endorsement—confirm requirements with a licensed representative.

### 
				 
				 If my car is stolen, what should I do first?

What to do first

- Start tracking: If you have a tracking device, contact the provider immediately.
					 
- Report to police: File a report as soon as you suspect theft.
					 
- Check towing: Look for tow/parking signs and call local towing to rule out a tow.
					 
- Call your insurer: Open a claim and get guidance on next steps.

Have this ready

- Registration, driver's licence, purchase/rental contract.
					 
- Vehicle details (make, model, VIN) and keys.
					 
- Invoices for repairs/add-ons; a list of items that were in the car.

Coverage notes

- Auto insurance typically covers items used only with your car (e.g., installed or car-specific equipment).
					 
- Other personal items are generally covered under home or tenants insurance, depending on your coverage.

What happens next

A claims adjuster typically contacts you within 24–48 hours to complete your statement.

### 
				 
				 Does car insurance cover theft and vandalism?

You're generally covered for theft and vandalism when your policy includes comprehensive (or all perils) coverage.

Comprehensive covers non-collision events such as:

- Theft
					 
- Vandalism
					 
- Break-ins
					 
- Fire
					 
- Weather (e.g., hail)
					 
- Glass damage

Additional notes

- Comprehensive coverage usually has its own deductible. Collision covers crash/impact damage and doesn't apply to theft or vandalism.
					 
- Personal items taken from the car are typically not covered by auto insurance unless they're used only with the car; those are generally covered under home or tenants insurance, depending on your coverage.

Next step

To confirm what's included in your province and your current deductible, check your policy declarations or contact your insurer or a licensed belairdirect representative.

				 As my car gets older, why doesn’t my car insurance premium go down?

There are several factors we take into consideration when we calculate your car insurance premium. The two main factors are the cost of parts (which don’t always decrease, like the value of the vehicle), and the cost of the repairs (we need to pay the mechanics who repair your vehicle). ## Why Are Car Insurance Premiums Rising? Factors and Solutions
**URL:** https://www.belairdirect.com/blog/insurance-premium-increases-explained

### Understanding car insurance premium increase: what you need to know

You’ve probably noticed that insurance premiums are on the rise everywhere lately. Inevitably, this has affected the car insurance and home insurance sectors, since the rates that impact your insurance premiums are based on current market conditions.

### What causes car insurance premium increases? 

### 1. Climate change

High winds, rain and hail storms, forest fires… Climate change has more than doubled insurance claims due to severe weather conditions over the past 5 to 10 years. This means insurance companies are reimbursing four times more claims related to natural disasters today than they did in the 1980s.

This is why, in the near future, insurance companies will need to implement premium increases for customers. These increases will allow insurers to keep pace with the mounting number of claims and thus continue to adequately protect policyholders.

### 2. Increased theft

In 2022, the number of car thefts may well be the highest we’ve seen since 2012. Most vehicles are stolen from shopping centre and supermarket parking lots—surprisingly, mainly during the daytime. Insurance companies have had no choice but to adapt to this frustrating reality, including by implementing insurance premium increases.

### 3. An increase in the price of parts and labour

Have you tried to hire a tradesperson lately to replace a part on your vehicle? If so, you’ve probably had to add significantly to your repair budget. Here’s why: inflation and a growing labour shortage.

Unfortunately, this has also affected the suppliers who perform claims work for insurance companies. We now pay more to obtain their services. For example, even though the number of claims only rose by 3.4% in 2020, repair work rose by 11.2%. It’s an increase that unfortunately leads to insurance premium increases.

### 4. Increase in driving

At the start of the pandemic, some insurance companies—including belairdirect—adjusted rates to give customers some breathing room. But with things being largely back to “normal,” as well as all the factors listed above, these rates are being readjusted to reflect today’s new reality.

For a broader understanding of why auto insurance premiums have increased, visit the Insurance Bureau of Canada for more information. 

### How to manage your car insurance costs

There are several ways to reduce the cost of your car insurance premium. Here are some practical tips to help you save: 

- Increase your deductible: A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. By increasing your deductible, you can lower your monthly premium because you are taking on more of the initial cost in the event of a claim. Learn more about how insurance deductibles work. 

- Consider bundling your insurance: Combining multiple insurance products, like car and home insurance, with a single trusted insurer like belairdirect allows you to save both time and money. Bundling can lead to significant discounts on your premiums. Discover our insurance bundle options now. 

- Install a home security system: Installing a central alarm system can lower your home insurance premium. It shows your insurer that you're taking proactive steps to prevent loss from theft or damage caused by a home invasion, which reduces the risk for the insurer. 

- Drive together, save together: If multiple family members in your household drive, insuring all vehicles with the same company can lead to discounts. This consolidates your insurance and often results in a lower overall premium. 

- Have winter tires installed: Winter tires improve your vehicle’s safety in cold weather conditions. This can lead to premium reductions, as insurers recognize the reduced risk of accidents.  

- Usage-based insurance: With automerit, you can take control of your car insurance costs. This program adjusts your premiums based on your driving habits, potentially rewarding safe drivers with lower rates. 

- Group program: Joining a group insurance program can offer additional savings. These programs provide preferred group discounts for your car and/or home insurance on top of any other discounts you may qualify for. 

- Reduce your mileage: By driving less, you lower the likelihood of being involved in an accident. If possible, use public transportation or carpool to commute. Reduced mileage can lead to lower insurance premiums because it represents less risk. 

### Understanding your policy and its impact on premiums

Understanding your insurance policy and how it impacts your premiums is crucial. Your insurance premium isn’t random but is rigorously calculated by an actuarial process and a host of other criteria: 

- Policy coverage: The extent of your coverage will impact your premium. Comprehensive and collision coverage, for instance, will cost more than basic liability coverage. 

- Driving record: A clean driving record can significantly lower your premiums. Conversely, a history of accidents or traffic violations can increase your rates. 

- Vehicle type: The make and model of your vehicle can affect your insurance costs. Generally, more expensive and high-performance cars cost more to insure. 

- Usage and mileage: How often and how far you drive can influence your premium. Lower mileage can mean lower rates. 

- Location: Where you live and park your vehicle also plays a role. Areas with higher theft rates or more traffic can lead to higher premiums. 

Remember, there are multiple ways to manage and potentially lower your car insurance costs. If you have any questions about your insurance premium or need a personalized quote, don’t hesitate to get in touch with us. We’re here to help you understand and optimize your coverage.

### What are the types of car insurance coverage in Canada?

Every province in Canada has different requirements for car mandatory insurance coverage. For example, in Quebec the only mandatory coverage is Civil Liability Coverage. In Ontario and Alberta, all drivers are required by law to have Liability Coverage, Accident Benefits Coverage, Uninsured Motorist Coverage and Direct Compensation Property Damage.
